Citizenship:

The definition of citizenship is the status of being a citizen, along with the rights, duties and privileges of being a citizen (yourdictionary.com).

Characteristics of Citizenship:

  • Honesty
  • Compassion
  • Respect
  • Responsibility
  • Courage
  • Loyalty

Honesty:

The definition of honesty is the quality of being truthful (yourdictionary.com). Honesty is a necessary component for citizenship, because honesty determines how truthful somebody is. If you are always honest, you will have a good reputation of being credible.

Compassion:

Sorrow for the sufferings or trouble of another or others, accompanied by an urge to help; deep sympathy; pity (yourdictionary.com). Compassion is a necessary component for citizenship, because it shows how much a person cares about other people, animals, materialistic objects, etc.

Respect:

To feel or show honor or esteem for; hold in high regard (yourdictionary.com). Respect is a necessary component for citizenship, because it shows how well a person can respect views, different from their own.

Responsibility:

Condition, quality, fact, or instance of being responsible; obligation, accountability, dependability, etc. (yourdictionary.com). Responsibility is necessary for citizenship, because it shows trust and initiative in people.

Courage:

The attitude of facing and dealing with anything recognized as dangerous, difficult, or painful, instead of withdrawing from it; quality of being fearless or brave; valor(yourdictionary.com). Courage is necessary for citizenship, because it shows how people are able to complete difficult tasks without being scared or nervous.

Loyalty:

Ties an obligation of support and faithfulness to a person, government, cause, duty, etc. (yourdictionary.com). Loyalty is necessary for citizenship, because it shows how people can trust other people, working together to create a working community.
